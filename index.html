About GeekFEST

GeekFEST is MACET's premier tech event, bringing together the best and brightest in coding, design, and innovation.

Events Lineup

Join exciting competitions such as the DSA Round, CSS Challenge, Typing Competition, Tech Treasure Hunt, and the grand Hackathon!

2. DSA Showdown

Description:
This round will test participants' problem-solving skills with challenging questions based on Data Structures and Algorithms. Competitors will face a series of coding problems and will be required to write efficient code to solve them.
Objective:
Solve all the DSA problems within the given time limit, focusing on both correctness and efficiency. Faster and more optimal solutions will receive higher scores.

Rules:

  • No AI assistance (e.g., ChatGPT) or internet searches are allowed.
  • Participants will use only the programming environment provided to them.
  • Plagiarism or copying code will lead to disqualification.

3. StyleSprint: The CSS Challenge

Description:
Participants will embark on a thrilling adventure to solve a series of clues scattered across both physical and digital locations. Each clue will test logical thinking, code-breaking, and puzzle-solving skills, requiring participants to work through complex challenges to uncover the next location or task.
Objective: Reproduce the given design accurately, ensuring responsiveness and adherence to best practices in CSS. Creativity and functionality are key to earning extra points.

Rules:

  • No AI tools or internet searches are allowed.
  • Participants can only use basic web development tools.
  • The use of external libraries or frameworks is prohibited.

4. TypeFury: The Typing Competition

Description:
A high-speed typing contest where participants will be judged based on how quickly and accurately they can type sentences or paragraphs presented to them. This event emphasizes both speed and precision in typing.
Objective:
Type the provided text as fast as possible with minimal errors. The final score will be calculated based on both typing speed and accuracy.

Rules:

  • No use of AI or internet resources.
  • Participants can use their device

5. BrainTeK: The Tech Quiz

Description:
A multi-round quiz that covers a broad range of tech-related topics, including computer science, programming languages, and emerging technology trends. The competition will feature various rounds such as multiple-choice questions (MCQs), image - based questions, and rapid-fire rounds.
Objective:
Answer as many questions as possible to accumulate points, with a special emphasis on quick and accurate responses in the rapid-fire round.

Rules:

  • The quiz will have multiple rounds, including MCQs, picture and rapid-fire rounds.
  • No internet searches or AI tools can be used.
  • Collaboration between teams is strictly prohibited.
  • Teams will compete head-to-head, and the team with the most points at the end of all rounds will be declared the winner.

6. Code Strom: The Hackathon

Description:
A fast-paced hackathon event where teams will collaborate to develop a working prototype or solve a real-world problem using technology. The challenge may focus on areas like app development, web development, or addressing societal issues. Teams are encouraged to brainstorm creative solutions and build them within a limited time frame.
Objective:
Create an innovative solution from scratch within the time limit. Teams will present a functional prototype at the end of the event, and extra points will be awarded for creativity and the practical application of the solution.

Rules:

  • Teams must code their solution from scratch during the event; pre-built solutions are disqualified.
  • AI tools (e.g., ChatGPT, Copilot) are allowed only for brainstorming and ideation purposes, but not for writing code.
  • Internet searches are allowed for documentation and libraries, but any use of pre-existing codebases, templates, or libraries must be declared.
  • Each team will present their solution, and judges will evaluate the functionality, creativity, and impact of the project.
